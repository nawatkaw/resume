Lesson Learnt from Study Trips For Engagement & EnRichment (STEER) to Nepal

We immerse ourselves learning new cultures and traditions. I am glad to share with you my personal reflections from this trip.

CAPT STEER NEPAL TEAM 2019
On the way to Pokhara
Kathmandu Scenery

Every small actions matter

Chitwan National Reserve is located in the middle surrounded by villagers. Sometimes, wildlife animals came out of the reserve into the village to steal food and water, and hence disrupted the villagers’ livelihood. During our trip, we visited one organization, Ecoprise who builds a solar pump in the middle of Chitwan National Forest to solve this problem. This project was single handled by the Chief Executive Officer of the organisation.

Solar Pump for water delivery for animals
Solar cell for water pump
Pond for wildlife animals

He uses a solar pump and applies a simple concept of Pumps and Turbine, which I have learned in my Chemical Engineering Thermodynamics, to pump underground water for wildlife animals to drink. As a result, less wildlife animals are less likely to escape the nature reserve and disrupt the villagers’ livelihood. Despite it being a simple project, it has benefited the community in one way or another. It taught me that no matter how small the actions are, it can impact the community in one way or another.

Disconnect to connect with nature

During the trip, I did not gain stable internet access most of the time, so I had limited access to all my text messages and social media. It turned out to be a blessing in disguise! I used this opportunity to disconnect from the virtual world and enjoy the beautiful scenery around me.

Hiking to Ghandruk
Roadtrip to Ghandruk village
Hiking Scenery

I always took the window seat and watched the scenery passed by during all the long road trips. It was a breathtaking experience as I saw the local’s simple livelihood such as kids playing catch, hopscotch while parents were using old agriculture practices plowing the rice field with animals. Besides, I saw many mountain ranges and beautiful sunsets which took my breath away.

Roadtrip back to Pokhara
Morning at Ghadruk
Stroll in Nepal

At night up in the Bhujung Village where there was no light pollution, I managed to see a sky full of stars, which are shown in the photos below. All these unforgettable sceneries will not happen if I got stable internet access, and once you miss it, it’s gone forever.

Stars at Bhujung village

Go out and explore the world!

This study trip is an eye-opening experience for me. I did many things that I could not have done in Singapore such as trekking. I immersed myself in a new culture and traditions by trying out the locals’ cuisine and lifestyle.

There are many things in the world that we had not explored, and there are many things that we have not done. We just have to be brave, take the first step and wanderlust into a foreign land, just like what Elsa said in Frozen 2 – “Into the unknown”
